10 Must-Visit Hiking Trails for Summer 2026

Nature has a way of resetting the soul. As the snow melts and the alpine meadows begin to bloom, hikers across the country are lace up their boots and checking their gear lists. This year, the focus is on sustainable exploration and discovering the "hidden gems" of our national park system.

1. The High Sierra Loop, California

While the John Muir Trail gets all the glory, the High Sierra Loop offers equally stunning vistas with half the crowds. Expect crystal clear lakes and granite peaks that touch the clouds.

"The mountains are calling, and I must go." — John Muir

Remember to pack appropriately for variable weather. Even in July, Sierra storms can bring sudden temperature drops and hail.

2. Thunder Knob Trail, Washington

Located in the North Cascades, this trail is perfect for families and beginner hikers. The payoff is an incredible view of the turquoise Diablo Lake.
